# Research Notes

Synthro turns research-backed learning patterns into concrete HTML interactions.

| Principle | Evidence | Synthro pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Active recall | Dunlosky et al. 2013, Agarwal et al. 2021 | Quiz before reveal, diagnostic checks, recurring concept probes. |
| Spacing | Carpenter, Pan, and Butler 2022; Latimier et al. 2021 | Revisit weak concepts later in the lesson. |
| Worked examples | Atkinson et al. 2000; Muldner et al. 2023; Hoogerheide et al. 2023, “The effect of worked examples on learning solution steps and knowledge transfer” | Annotated PR walkthroughs with explicit solution steps, step recall checks, and near/far transfer prompts. |
| Faded practice | Weinman, Fox, and Hearst 2021 | Solved example, partial completion, independent task. |
| Generation | Bertsch et al. 2007; Schindler and Richter 2023 | Fill blanks, predict failures, label patterns, write summaries. |
| Self-explanation | Bisra et al. 2018; Vihavainen, Miller, and Settle 2015 | Explain-why prompts and review rationale. |
| Interleaving | Brunmair and Richter 2019 | Contrast cards for confusable engineering patterns. |
| Transfer | Pan and Rickard 2018 | Final mission in a new but related repo context. |
| Cognitive load | Berssanette and de Francisco 2021 | Progressive reveal, code next to diagrams, small steps. |
| Deliberate practice | Ericsson et al. 1993; Macnamara et al. 2014 | Focused reps with feedback and difficulty calibration. |

Use these as product rules, not citations sprinkled after the fact. The interaction planner should choose an exercise type because it supports a learning goal.
